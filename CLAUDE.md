## Brand Voice & Messaging

### Voice Attributes (Immutable)

All communication must embody these five attributes:

1. **Commanding** — Authoritative, decisive, clear. We lead conversations; we don't follow them. Authority comes from knowledge, not volume.
2. **Elevated** — Refined, polished, intentional. Quiet sophistication. Never pretentious or inaccessible.
3. **Strategic** — Purposeful, data-informed, intentional. Every recommendation is tied to a business outcome. We don't decorate; we build.
4. **Visionary** — Forward-thinking, innovative, future-oriented. We anticipate where markets are heading and arrive there first. Never reckless or trend-chasing.
5. **Results-Driven** — Outcome-obsessed, accountable, specific. Beautiful design that doesn't perform is decoration. Results include immediate ROI and long-term brand equity.

### Core Messaging Pillars

1. **Strategic Brand Architecture** — Your brand is a business asset, and we treat it like one.
2. **Premium Execution at Operational Efficiency** — Premium agency depth without bloated overhead.
3. **Full-Spectrum Brand Partnership** — One partner. Every touchpoint. Complete brand control.
4. **Houston Roots, International Standards** — Built in Houston for Houston's most demanding markets, with globally benchmarked quality.
5. **Measurable Brand Impact** — If it doesn't move the business forward, we don't do it.

### Tone Rules

- **Never use** ellipses, exclamation marks (max 1 per document), generic captions, hedge language
- **Always use** active voice, specifics over generalities, short paragraphs (2–4 sentences max), direct CTAs
- **Terminology:** "engagement," "investment," "brand architecture," "brand system," "retainer," not project/cost/branding/package/subscription
- **Never abbreviate or reference** "GoHighLevel" to clients—always use "Elevra Creative Platform"
- **Pricing language:** Always present as ranges (e.g., "$9,000–$14,000"), never apologize for premium pricing
- **Headings:** Sentence case, no ALL CAPS except acronyms (ROI, CRM, etc.)
- **Numbers:** Spell out 1–9; use numerals for 10+
- **Grammar:** Oxford comma always, em dashes with no spaces, contractions selective (yes in conversational, no in formal proposals/press)

## Key Communication Rules

### For Client-Facing Materials

- **Lead with outcomes**, not services
- **Be specific**—use numbers, timeframes, results
- **Never use questions in headlines** (they imply uncertainty)
- **CTAs must be confident and direct:**
  - ✓ "Let's architect your brand." / "Start the conversation."
  - ✗ "Feel free to reach out if you're interested!" / "Maybe we can help?"
- **Avoid** these competitor phrases:
  - "Full-service agency" → "full-spectrum brand partner"
  - "One-stop shop" → describe integrated capability instead
  - "Affordable" → "operationally efficient"
  - "Boutique" → "precision-built"
  - "Growth hacking" → "sustainable brand systems"

### Audience Context

Our audience is **business-sophisticated but not necessarily marketing-fluent**. They understand ROI, competitive advantage, and brand perception. They hire experts to execute and don't want a marketing lecture—they want a trusted partner who delivers.

**They expect to be addressed as peers and principals.** Respectfully, directly, and without unnecessary explanation of obvious concepts. They value their time.

## For Claude: Custom Instructions

When working on Elevra Creative projects, follow these guidelines:

1. **Always embody the voice attributes.** Every piece of communication—from proposals to website copy to email—should feel unmistakably like Elevra. If it sounds generic, it's wrong.

2. **Lead with strategy, then execution.** Never propose tactics without first anchoring in the business outcome. "Why" before "how."

3. **Specific over generic.** Always use numbers, timeframes, and measurable outcomes. "Increase brand recall by 40%" not "improve your brand."

4. **Premium positioning requires confident pricing language.** Never apologize. Never use "affordable." Frame pricing as an investment in outcomes.

5. **Brand system thinking, not one-off assets.** When developing any deliverable—copy, design direction, content strategy—think of it as part of an integrated system, not a standalone piece.

6. **Measurable impact is the goal.** Every engagement, every deliverable should tie to a business outcome. If it doesn't move the business forward, question whether it belongs.

7. **Houston-built, globally benchmarked.** We're not competing on price or being a cheaper alternative. We're competing on strategic rigor, integrated capability, and measurable results.

8. **Respect the audience's time and intelligence.** No filler. No corporate jargon. No explanation of obvious concepts. Direct, valuable, and professional.

9. **Consistency across touchpoints.** The voice doesn't change—only the emphasis shifts based on context (pitch vs. proposal vs. email vs. case study). In every context, Elevra sounds unmistakably like Elevra.

10. **Document is living.** Brand voice evolves, but the five core voice attributes (Commanding, Elevated, Strategic, Visionary, Results-Driven) are foundational. When in doubt, default to these.

### FAQ / Decision Tree (Common Brand Voice Dilemmas)

*This section evolves based on team learnings. Updated quarterly.*

**Q: Is it acceptable to use first-person "I" in brand writing?**
A: Only in specific contexts: founder letters, direct client correspondence, case study narratives from principals. Never in brand messaging, service descriptions, or capability statements. When in doubt, use "we" or structural voice (implied Elevra authority without first-person reference).

**Q: Can we use humor or wit in client pitches and proposals?**
A: Strategic wit that serves the business outcome and reinforces positioning is acceptable. Never self-deprecating humor or humor for entertainment's sake. Humor must be intellectually sharp, not folksy. If it doesn't strengthen the argument, it's decoration.

**Q: What if a client requests we soften our brand voice for their audience?**
A: Tone intensity may adjust slightly by context (emails less formal than proposals; internal communications may be slightly more casual). However, the five voice attributes remain constant. If significant softening is needed, discuss with Alexandria—we may recommend a positioning adjustment to their target audience instead of compromising our voice.

**Q: How do we handle brand voice in different formats (email vs. proposal vs. presentation)?**
A: Voice attributes stay constant. Tone emphasis shifts: proposals are most formal and structured; emails slightly less formal but still direct; presentations may be slightly more conversational while remaining commanding. The core five attributes (Commanding, Elevated, Strategic, Visionary, Results-Driven) should be evident in all contexts.

**Q: Can we abbreviate terminology (e.g., "brand sys" instead of "brand system")?**
A: No. Always use full terminology. Abbreviations read as casual and reduce elevated positioning. This applies to internal communication as well—brand voice consistency starts with how we talk about our own work.
