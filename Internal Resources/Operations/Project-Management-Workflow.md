## STAGE 3: ACTIVE WORK

### Project Phases (Per SOW)

Work is divided into phases, each with:
- Specific deliverables
- Deadline
- Approval gate
- Success criteria

**Example phases:**
1. Research & Discovery (1 week)
2. Strategy Development (2 weeks)
3. Design/Execution (2–3 weeks)
4. Revision & Refinement (1–2 weeks)
5. Final Approval & Launch Prep (1 week)

*(Customize per project type)*

---

### Phase Workflow

**For each phase:**

1. **Phase kickoff**
   - Team understands deliverables and deadline
   - Dependencies identified ("Do we have what we need to start?")
   - Risks flagged ("What could delay us?")

2. **Work in progress**
   - Team executes per SOP and Creative Brief
   - Daily standups (15 min, if complex project)
   - Flag blockers immediately
   - Maintain quality standards

3. **Internal review (QA)**
   - Work reviewed against Creative Brief and quality standards
   - Brand voice verified (is this Elevra?)
   - All deliverables complete? All specs met?
   - Issues logged, rework planned if needed

4. **Client delivery**
   - Deliverables packaged and shared
   - Client has X days to review and provide feedback
   - Status communicated to client (email/meeting)

5. **Feedback incorporation**
   - Client feedback received (within deadline)
   - Feedback reviewed for in-scope vs. out-of-scope
   - Adjustments made per feedback (round 1 of 2)
   - Revised deliverables resubmitted

---

### Revision Process

**Revision Round 1:** Initial feedback and changes

- Client provides feedback on deliverables (form: email, shared doc, or call)
- Feedback consolidated (all at once, not scattered)
- Team reviews and prioritizes feedback
- [X days] to revise and resubmit
- Revised deliverables presented

**Revision Round 2:** Refinements and final tweaks

- Client provides second round of feedback (if needed)
- More targeted feedback expected (not major changes)
- [X days] to refine
- Final version presented

**Beyond Round 2:** Scope change

- Any additional revisions beyond Round 2 are out of scope
- Quoted at $[X]/hour or discussed as change order
- Change order signed before additional work begins

**Approval:**
- Client approves deliverable in writing (email acknowledgment sufficient)
- Phase complete, move to next phase

## STAGE 4: QUALITY ASSURANCE & APPROVAL

### Pre-Delivery Review

**Before final delivery to client:**

- [ ] **Design/Code QA**
  - All specs met? (colors, fonts, sizing, etc.)
  - No errors or typos? (designs, copy, code)
  - Brand voice consistent? (tone, messaging, visual)
  - Accessibility standards met? (if web)
  - Performance optimized? (if digital)

- [ ] **Deliverable Package Review**
  - All files organized and named correctly
  - All formats correct (PDF, SVG, source files, etc.)
  - Documentation complete (guidelines, specs, usage)
  - No missing pieces or incomplete work

- [ ] **Brand Voice Check**
  - Does this feel unmistakably like Elevra?
  - Commanding, elevated, strategic, visionary, results-driven?
  - Alexandria spot-check review if strategic project

- [ ] **Approval Confirmation**
  - Final approval from project lead
  - Alexandria approval (if required per project)
  - Client final approval scheduled

### Approval Gate

**Final approval meeting/call (if appropriate):**

- Project Lead or Alexandria walks through final deliverables
- Address any final questions
- Get client sign-off: "Yes, this is approved"
- Confirm next steps (launch, implementation, training)

## ESCALATION & ISSUE MANAGEMENT

### Issue Types & Escalation

#### Timeline Slippage

**Issue:** Deliverable will miss deadline

**Timeline:** Flag within 24 hours of realization (not at deadline)

**Escalation path:**
1. Project Manager identifies delay
2. Project Lead assesses cause and recovery plan
3. If <2 days delay: PM communicates revised date to client
4. If >2 days delay: Enrique involved, may require SOW amendment
5. If critical path threatened: Enrique escalates to Alexandria

**Communication to Client:**
- Project Manager sends email ASAP
- New timeline provided with reason
- Recovery plan if applicable
- Escalated decision if major impact

#### Scope Creep / Change Request

**Issue:** Client asks for work beyond original SOW

**Process:**
1. Project Manager flags as scope change
2. Project Lead confirms in/out of scope
3. Enrique evaluates scope impact and cost
4. If approved: Change order created (additional fee or timeline)
5. Client confirms approval and fees before work begins

**Communication to Client:**
- "That's a great idea and it's outside the original scope. Here's what that would look like in terms of time/cost..."
- Change order presented with clear deliverables and fees

#### Quality Issues

**Issue:** Work doesn't meet standards or brand voice

**Process:**
1. Project Lead identifies issue during QA
2. Root cause assessed
3. Rework plan created
4. Issue logged but NOT sent to client (internal quality issue)
5. Resubmitted to client as refinement (not disclosed as error)
6. If major issue: Escalated to Alexandria

#### Approval Delays

**Issue:** Client hasn't provided feedback or approval for X days

**Timeline:**
- Expected feedback window: [X business days]
- After [X] days: PM sends reminder email
- After [X+5] days: PM + Project Lead reach out
- After [X+10] days: Enrique reaches out (escalation)

**Impact on Timeline:**
- Timeline extends by delay length (client responsible)
- Launch date adjusted accordingly
- Communicated clearly to client

#### Team Issues (Sickness, Turnover, Capacity)

**Issue:** Team member unavailable, deliverable at risk

**Process:**
1. Immediate notification to Enrique
2. Assess impact: Can remaining team handle? Need to hire/subcontract?
3. Client communication: Transparency about plan to stay on schedule
4. Mitigation: Overtime, subcontracting, or timeline adjustment
5. Keep client informed of resolution

## COMMUNICATION STANDARDS

### Client Communication

**Tone:** Always Elevra voice (Commanding, Elevated, Strategic, Visionary, Results-Driven)

**Frequency:**
- Weekly status update (Friday)
- Ad-hoc for major updates or blockers
- Scheduled calls per kickoff agreement

**Channels:**
- Email (formal updates, approvals)
- Slack (if mutually agreed)
- Scheduled calls (strategy, approvals, feedback)
- Shared project tracker (ongoing visibility)

**Rules:**
- All communication from Project Manager or Project Lead (not entire team)
- No bad news by surprise (flag issues early)
- No overpromising (be realistic about timelines)
- Always assume they're busy (respect their time)
- Be specific (not vague)

### Internal Communication

**Daily:**
- Slack in project channel for quick updates
- Flag blockers immediately (don't wait for standup)

**Weekly:**
- [Day/Time] project standup (15 min)
- What's done, what's next, any blockers
- Quick escalations

**Issues:**
- Escalate to Project Lead or Enrique within same day
- Don't wait for meetings to share problems

## BEST PRACTICES

### Planning

- ✓ Break work into smaller chunks (not one giant deliverable)
- ✓ Build in buffer time (not padding to billing)
- ✓ Identify dependencies upfront
- ✓ Confirm client availability (review cycles, approvals)

### Execution

- ✓ Update PM tool daily (it's the source of truth)
- ✓ Flag issues early (don't surprise at deadline)
- ✓ Communicate frequently (weekly minimum)
- ✓ Quality gate every deliverable (QA before client sees)
- ✓ Respect deadlines (meet them or communicate early)

### Client Relationships

- ✓ Lead, don't follow (make strategic recommendations)
- ✓ Be helpful (answer questions, provide guidance)
- ✓ Be honest (if they're wrong, tell them respectfully)
- ✓ Respect their time (efficient meetings, clear communication)
- ✓ Follow through (do what you say)
