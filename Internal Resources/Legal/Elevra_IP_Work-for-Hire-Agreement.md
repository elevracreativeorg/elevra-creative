# IP & Work-for-Hire Agreement
## Intellectual Property & Creative Ownership

**Elevra Creative, LLC**

---

## PURPOSE

This IP & Work-for-Hire Agreement ("**Agreement**") establishes clear ownership of all creative work, intellectual property, and deliverables produced by Elevra Creative for clients, and ensures that all team members and contractors understand work product ownership and usage rights.

---

## 1. WORK-FOR-HIRE DEFINITION

All creative work, deliverables, designs, code, copy, photography direction, strategy documents, and any other work product produced by Elevra or Elevra team members ("**Work Product**") is considered "work made for hire" under the Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. § 101).

Unless otherwise specified in a signed Statement of Work (SOW), Work Product ownership is initially vested in **Elevra Creative, LLC** and then transferred to the Client upon full payment of all fees.

---

## 2. OWNERSHIP TRANSFER

### Initial Ownership

Upon creation, all Work Product is owned by Elevra Creative. This includes:

- ✓ Custom designs (logos, visual identities, layouts, brand systems)
- ✓ Custom code (websites, applications, integrations, plugins)
- ✓ Strategy documents (positioning frameworks, messaging guides, content strategies)
- ✓ Original photography direction and shot lists
- ✓ Video direction, editing, and production assets
- ✓ Copywriting and editorial content
- ✓ Any custom tools, templates, or frameworks developed specifically for the project

### Transfer to Client

Ownership of all custom Work Product transfers to Client **upon full payment** of all fees and expenses outlined in the Statement of Work. This transfer is exclusive and irrevocable.

**Conditional Transfer:** If Client terminates engagement or fails to pay outstanding invoices, ownership remains with Elevra until all fees are paid in full.

---

## 3. ELEVRA'S RETAINED RIGHTS

Even after ownership transfer to Client, Elevra retains the following rights:

### Portfolio & Case Study Rights

Elevra retains the right to:
- Display the Work Product in Elevra's portfolio and case studies
- Reference the Client's name and describe the project in public materials
- Use anonymized metrics and results for marketing purposes
- Photograph and document the work for showcase and award submissions

**Client Approval:** Elevra requests Client's permission before featuring work in public-facing marketing, but this is courtesy not a requirement. Client may request anonymization of sensitive information.

### Reusable Assets & Methodologies

Elevra retains ownership of all pre-existing or general-purpose assets used in the Work Product, including:
- Design templates, component libraries, and reusable design systems
- Methodologies, frameworks, and strategic processes
- Client-agnostic tools, scripts, and code libraries
- Photography or video not created specifically for the Client project

**Client License:** Client receives a non-exclusive license to use Elevra's reusable assets solely as they appear in the final Work Product delivered. Client may not extract, reverse-engineer, or separately license these assets.

---

## 4. THIRD-PARTY CONTENT & LICENSES

### Stock Assets & Licensed Content

If the Work Product includes third-party assets (stock photos, icons, fonts, music, video clips), Client is responsible for:
- Understanding the license terms of each asset
- Obtaining appropriate licenses (Elevra will facilitate)
- Adhering to usage restrictions (personal use, commercial use, attribution, etc.)
- Paying for extended or commercial licenses if needed

**Elevra's Role:** Elevra sources and integrates third-party content on Client's behalf but does not warrant that licenses are perpetual or exclusive.

### Attribution & Credits

Client agrees to maintain attribution and credit lines for:
- Elevra Creative (visible credit in website footer, portfolio, case study, or marketing materials)
- Any third-party creators or licenses that require attribution
- Open-source software or libraries used in the Work Product

---

## 5. PRE-EXISTING ELEVRA IP

The following remain Elevra's exclusive property and are **not transferred** to Client:

- ✓ Elevra's proprietary brand voice guide, positioning frameworks, and strategic methodologies
- ✓ Elevra's design systems, component libraries, and reusable design assets (unless specially created for this project)
- ✓ Elevra's software tools, automation systems, and proprietary platforms (e.g., Elevra Creative Platform)
- ✓ Elevra's client list, business practices, and operational processes
- ✓ General knowledge, skills, and expertise gained during the engagement
- ✓ Any code, tools, or systems developed for Elevra's internal use

**Client License:** Client receives a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use Elevra's pre-existing IP solely as incorporated into the Work Product delivered.

---

## 6. MORAL RIGHTS

Where permitted by law, Elevra waives all moral rights claims (including right of attribution and right of integrity) to Work Product, except:

- **Attribution in Portfolio:** Elevra may credit itself as the creator in case studies, awards, and public portfolio display
- **Integrity Protection:** Elevra may decline to have its name associated with the work if Client makes alterations that materially change the creative intent

Client may not use Elevra's name or likeness in any way that is false, defamatory, or misleading.

---

## 7. TEAM MEMBERS & CONTRACTORS

### Employee Work Product

All Work Product created by Elevra employees during their employment is owned by Elevra. Employees assign all rights in Work Product to Elevra and acknowledge that work is created as part of their job responsibilities.

**Employee Signature Required:** All Elevra employees must sign an acknowledgment of this agreement as part of their employment.

### Independent Contractors & Subcontractors

Elevra engages independent contractors (designers, developers, photographers) to assist with work. All contractors must:

- Sign Elevra's Independent Contractor Agreement confirming that Work Product is owned by Elevra
- Assign all intellectual property rights in their work to Elevra
- Acknowledge that Elevra may transfer these rights to Client
- Agree that their work is confidential and may not be used for other purposes without Elevra's consent

**Elevra's Responsibility:** Elevra is responsible for ensuring all contractors execute work-for-hire agreements and IP assignment documents.

---

## 8. CLIENT-PROVIDED MATERIALS

If Client provides pre-existing materials, assets, or content for use in the Work Product (logos, photography, copy, data), Client warrants that:

- ✓ Client owns or has licensed the materials
- ✓ Client has permission to use the materials in the scope of this engagement
- ✓ Client has obtained all necessary third-party permissions or licenses
- ✓ Client indemnifies Elevra against any claims that Client's materials infringe third-party rights

**Elevra's Use:** Elevra may use Client-provided materials solely to create the Work Product. Elevra does not claim ownership of Client's pre-existing materials.

---

## 9. JOINT OWNERSHIP & COLLABORATION

If Client provides significant creative input, direction, or source materials that materially influence the final Work Product, the parties may agree to **joint ownership** or **shared licensing rights**. This must be explicitly stated in the Statement of Work (SOW).

**Default:** In the absence of a specific provision in the SOW, Client owns the final Work Product and Elevra retains portfolio and methodology rights.

---

## 10. PAYMENT & IP TRANSFER

IP ownership transfer is conditional on **full payment** of all fees and expenses.

If Client fails to pay:
- ✓ Elevra retains ownership of all Work Product
- ✓ Work Product may not be used or distributed by Client
- ✓ Elevra may use the Work Product for other purposes
- ✓ Client remains liable for payment of all invoices

Upon payment in full, Elevra provides written confirmation of IP transfer (if requested).

---

## 11. INDEMNIFICATION

**Elevra's Indemnity:** Elevra indemnifies Client against any third-party claims that Work Product created solely by Elevra infringes third-party intellectual property rights (excluding Client-provided content).

**Client's Indemnity:** Client indemnifies Elevra against any claims arising from Client's use, modification, or integration of Work Product, including claims related to Client-provided content.

**Exceptions:** Indemnification does not apply if Work Product is modified by parties other than Elevra without Elevra's consent.

---

## 12. CONFIDENTIALITY OF WORK PRODUCT

During development, all Work Product is confidential. Client agrees not to:
- Disclose work-in-progress to competitors or third parties without Elevra's consent
- Share pre-launch designs or strategies without written permission
- Use incomplete Work Product without Elevra's approval

Post-delivery, Client owns the Work Product and may use, modify, or share at Client's discretion.

---

## 13. TERMINATION & WORK PRODUCT

If engagement is terminated before completion:

- Elevra retains ownership of all incomplete Work Product
- Client receives ownership of completed Work Product (deliverables accepted and paid for)
- Elevra may use abandoned Work Product for other purposes or portfolio display
- Client remains liable for payment of work completed through termination date

---

## 14. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

If there is a dispute regarding IP ownership, authorship, or rights:

1. Parties will attempt to resolve the dispute in writing within 30 days
2. If unresolved, dispute will be escalated to Alexandria France (Elevra) and Client's authorized representative
3. If still unresolved, parties agree to binding arbitration under Texas law

---

## 15. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Texas, including provisions of the Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. § 201(b) - work made for hire).
