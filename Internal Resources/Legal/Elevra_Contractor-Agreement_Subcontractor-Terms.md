## 3. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

Contractor is an independent contractor, **not** an employee of Elevra. Contractor:

- ✓ Controls the manner and means of performance
- ✓ Is responsible for all employment and payroll taxes

## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY & WORK PRODUCT

### Ownership of Work Product

All creative work, deliverables, designs, code, copy, photography, video, and any other work product produced by Contractor ("**Work Product**") is owned by Elevra and will be transferred to Elevra's client (or used internally by Elevra).

**Work-for-Hire:** Contractor acknowledges that all Work Product is "work made for hire" under the Copyright Act and shall be owned by Elevra upon creation.

**IP Assignment:** Contractor hereby assigns to Elevra all right, title, and interest in the Work Product, including all intellectual property rights (copyright, patents, trademarks).

### Contractor's Retained Rights

Contractor retains rights to:
- Pre-existing work, tools, or methodologies created prior to this engagement
- General knowledge, skills, and experience gained during the engagement
- Work created on Contractor's own time using Contractor's own resources that is unrelated to Services

Contractor may not use or disclose Elevra's confidential information or Client information for other purposes.

### Client-Facing Attribution

Unless specifically instructed otherwise:
- Elevra may credit Contractor's name and discipline in case studies or portfolio materials
- Elevra may anonymize Contractor's role if requested
- Client may not credit Contractor directly without Elevra's written consent (Client credits Elevra)

## 5. CONFIDENTIALITY & NON-DISCLOSURE

Contractor agrees to keep all confidential information confidential, including:

- Client names, strategies, and business information
- Elevra's business practices, client list, and proprietary methods
- Project details, deliverables, and work-in-progress
- Any information marked "Confidential" or that is reasonably identifiable as confidential

**Duration:** Confidentiality obligations survive termination for three (3) years.

**Permitted Disclosure:** Contractor may disclose information if:
- Required by law or court order (with prior notice to Elevra when possible)
- Disclosed with Elevra's prior written consent
- Disclosed in generic terms as part of portfolio work samples (anonymized)

**No Recruitment:** Contractor agrees not to recruit or solicit Elevra employees during or for one (1) year after engagement without Elevra's consent.

## 8. TERMINATION

### Termination for Convenience

Either party may terminate this engagement with written notice. If Contractor terminates:
- Contractor must provide reasonable notice (at least 7 days for ongoing work)
- Elevra pays only for completed work delivered and accepted
- Elevra is not liable for lost future income or damages

If Elevra terminates:
- Elevra pays Contractor for all completed work (accepted deliverables) and reasonable work-in-progress
- Elevra owns all completed Work Product
- Contractor returns all Client materials and confidential information

### Termination for Cause

Elevra may terminate immediately if Contractor:
- Violates terms of this Agreement
- Fails to deliver acceptable work
- Breaches confidentiality
- Engages in illegal or unethical conduct

Upon termination for cause, Elevra is not obligated to pay for incomplete work.
