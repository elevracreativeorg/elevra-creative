# Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation Agreement
## Employee Restrictive Covenants

**Elevra Creative, LLC**

---

## PURPOSE

This Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation Agreement ("**Agreement**") is entered into to protect Elevra Creative's legitimate business interests, including its confidential information, client relationships, and competitive position. It applies to all employees upon hire.

---

## 1. NON-COMPETE COVENANT

### Restricted Activity

During employment and for **one (1) year** after termination (the "**Restricted Period**"), Employee agrees not to, directly or indirectly:

- Engage in any business that competes with Elevra's services
- Solicit or accept work from Elevra clients
- Perform services for competitors in Elevra's target industries
- Develop competing brand, design, or digital services

### Definition of "Competing Business"

A competing business provides any of Elevra's full-spectrum services:
- Brand architecture and strategy
- Visual identity and design systems
- Website development and digital strategy
- Content production (photography, video, editorial)
- Paid media and advertising services
- CRM and marketing automation

### Geographic Scope

The non-compete applies to:
- **Primary market:** Houston, Texas and surrounding metro areas
- **Secondary markets:** Any market where Elevra has active clients or prospects
- **National scope:** If Employee works on national/remote clients

If geographic restriction is deemed unenforceable, it's limited to the **primary market (Houston metro)**.

### Permitted Activities

**NOT prohibited:**
- ✓ Consulting or freelance work unrelated to Elevra's services
- ✓ Employment in non-competing industries (e.g., manufacturing, finance, healthcare)
- ✓ Personal hobbies or side projects unrelated to Elevra's services

**Permitted after employment ends:**
- ✓ Working for non-competing companies in any industry
- ✓ Starting a business in a different industry

---

## 2. NON-SOLICITATION OF CLIENTS

### Client Non-Solicitation

During employment and for **two (2) years** after termination, Employee agrees not to:

- Solicit, encourage, or accept business from Elevra clients (known and prospective)
- Offer competing services to Elevra clients
- Redirect Elevra client work to other vendors
- Use Elevra client relationships for personal benefit

**Definition of "Elevra Client":** Any company, individual, or entity with whom Elevra has:
- Active service engagement
- Signed contract (regardless of status)
- Engaged in meaningful discussions within past 2 years
- Identified as prospective client

### Client Contact Restrictions

For **two (2) years** after termination, Employee shall not:
- Contact Elevra clients (by phone, email, social media, in-person)
- Accept inquiries from Elevra clients
- Respond to client outreach
- Meet with clients for business purposes

**Exception:** If contacted by a client for reasons unrelated to Elevra (e.g., personal friendship, social event), Employee may maintain personal relationship but must not solicit business.

---

## 3. NON-SOLICITATION OF EMPLOYEES

### Employee Non-Solicitation

During employment and for **one (1) year** after termination, Employee agrees not to:

- Recruit, solicit, or encourage Elevra employees to leave
- Hire Elevra employees or contract with them
- Facilitate employment elsewhere for Elevra team members
- Communicate with Elevra staff about job opportunities (except casual personal conversation)

This applies to:
- **Direct communication:** Calling, emailing, messaging employees
- **Indirect recruitment:** Using recruiters or third parties
- **Enticement:** Offering better pay, benefits, or opportunities as lure

**Exception:** If an Elevra employee independently initiates contact seeking employment, Employee may consider the opportunity without violating this covenant, provided Employee does not solicit the employee.

---

## 4. CONFIDENTIALITY & TRADE SECRETS

### Confidential Information (During & After Employment)

Employee acknowledges that Elevra's confidential information includes:

- Client lists and client contact information
- Pricing models and fee structures
- Client strategies, objectives, and business data
- Elevra's proprietary methodologies and frameworks
- Financial information and business performance
- Internal communications and strategic plans
- Vendor and partner relationships
- Password and system access information

### Obligations

Employee shall:
- ✓ Protect confidential information with reasonable care
- ✓ Not disclose to unauthorized parties
- ✓ Not use for personal benefit
- ✓ Return all confidential materials upon termination

### Duration

Confidentiality obligations continue **indefinitely** for:
- Trade secrets
- Client confidential information
- Proprietary methodologies

And for **three (3) years** post-termination for other confidential information.

---

## 5. RETURN OF MATERIALS

### Upon Termination

Employee shall immediately return:
- Company laptop, phone, equipment
- All files, documents, emails, projects
- Client materials and assets
- Login credentials and access information
- Any Elevra property or materials

### Digital Assets

Employee shall:
- Delete Elevra files from personal devices
- Confirm no copies retained
- Transfer ownership of work-related accounts (social, project tools, etc.)
- Provide all passwords and access

---

## 6. INJUNCTIVE RELIEF

### Irreparable Harm

Employee acknowledges that breach of this Agreement causes irreparable harm that cannot be remedied by monetary damages alone. Therefore:

- Elevra is entitled to seek **injunctive relief** (court orders) to prevent breach
- Employee waives the requirement to post a bond
- Injunctive relief is in addition to (not instead of) other remedies

### Specific Performance

Elevra may seek court orders requiring Employee to:
- Cease competitive activity
- Return confidential information
- Cease client solicitation
- Cease employee solicitation

---

## 7. CONSIDERATION & ENFORCEABILITY

### Consideration

Employee acknowledges this Agreement is supported by:
- Employment with Elevra
- Access to confidential information and client relationships
- Investment in Employee's skills and training
- Opportunity to work with premium clients

**Prior Employees:** For employees hired before this Agreement, continued employment constitutes consideration.

### Reasonableness

Employee acknowledges this Agreement's restrictions are:
- **Reasonable in duration** — Non-compete (1 year), non-solicitation (1–2 years)
- **Reasonable in scope** — Limited to Elevra's actual services and markets
- **Reasonable in geography** — Limited to primary market or national scope
- **Necessary to protect** — Legitimate business interests, client relationships, investments

### Blue Pencil Doctrine

If any provision is found unreasonable or unenforceable, the parties agree the court may modify the provision to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable (rather than striking it entirely).

---

## 8. TOLLING

If Employee breaches this Agreement or takes illegal action to avoid its terms, the Restricted Period is **extended** by the duration of the violation.

Example: If Employee solicits a client on month 11 of the 12-month non-compete, the non-compete period extends by an additional 12 months from the date of violation.

---

## 9. REMEDIES

### Available Remedies

In addition to injunctive relief, Elevra may seek:

- **Damages:** Monetary compensation for harm (client loss, reputation damage, etc.)
- **Disgorgement:** Recovery of profits Employee earned through breach
- **Attorney Fees:** Elevra may recover legal costs if it prevails
- **Liquidated Damages:** Pre-agreed amount if specified in employment contract

### No Waiver

Elevra's failure to enforce this Agreement does not waive the right to enforce it later.

---

## 10. SEVERABILITY

If any provision is found unenforceable:
- That provision is modified to the minimum extent to make it enforceable
- Remaining provisions remain in full force
- The parties' intent to protect Elevra's interests is preserved

**Jurisdiction:** Both parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction in state and federal courts in Harris County, Texas for any disputes.

## NOTES FOR TEXAS EMPLOYERS

This Agreement is designed to enforce under Texas law (Texas Business & Commerce Code § 15.50 and § 15.51). Texas requires restrictive covenants to be:

1. **Ancillary to lawful relationship** — ✓ Employment relationship
2. **Reasonable in temporal scope** — ✓ 1–2 years (reasonable per Texas case law)
3. **Reasonable in area** — ✓ Limited to Houston metro or national scope
4. **Reasonable in line of business** — ✓ Limited to services Elevra actually provides
5. **Legitimate business interest** — ✓ Protects client relationships and confidential information

This Agreement meets all Texas requirements for enforceability.
