## 1. PURPOSE

This NDA is entered into in connection with potential or existing business discussions and services relating to [describe general scope of engagement: e.g., "brand architecture and website development services"] (the "**Purpose**").

---

## 2. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION DEFINED

"**Confidential Information**" means all non-public, proprietary information disclosed by one Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party (the "**Receiving Party**"), whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, including:

**From Client to Elevra:**
- Business strategy, marketing plans, and competitive positioning
- Financial data, revenue figures, customer acquisition costs, and pricing
- Customer lists, client names, user data, and business metrics
- Product roadmaps, development plans, and technical architecture
- Proprietary processes, methodologies, and trade secrets
- Design concepts, brand assets, and creative work-in-progress

**From Elevra to Client:**
- Elevra's design methodologies, brand strategy frameworks, and creative processes
- Elevra's pricing models, engagement structures, and business practices
- Elevra's client list (names of other clients served)
- Proprietary tools, templates, and reusable assets developed by Elevra

---

## 3. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

The following information is **not** considered Confidential Information:

(a) **Public Domain:** Information that is, at the time of disclosure or thereafter becomes, publicly available through no breach of this NDA;

(b) **Prior Knowledge:** Information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate was known prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

(c) **Independent Development:** Information that the Receiving Party independently develops without reference to or use of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

(d) **Third-Party Receipt:** Information lawfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without confidentiality obligations;

(e) **Required Disclosure:** Information that the Receiving Party is legally required to disclose by law, court order, or government agency (provided Receiving Party gives Disclosing Party reasonable notice to seek protective order).

---

## 4. USE & PROTECTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**Permitted Use:** Receiving Party may use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose stated above and not for any other business purpose.

**Protection Obligation:** Receiving Party agrees to protect Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care. This includes:

- Limiting access to employees, contractors, and advisors who have a legitimate need to know
- Requiring those individuals to agree to confidentiality obligations
- Not disclosing Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
- Implementing reasonable security measures to prevent unauthorized access or disclosure

**No License or Rights:** Receipt of Confidential Information does not grant Receiving Party any license, ownership rights, or other proprietary interest in the Confidential Information.

---

## 5. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Upon request by the Disclosing Party, or upon termination of the business relationship, the Receiving Party agrees to:

- Return all documents, files, and materials containing Confidential Information; OR
- Destroy all Confidential Information and certify in writing that destruction is complete

**Exception:** Receiving Party may retain one copy of Confidential Information for archival and legal compliance purposes, provided it remains subject to confidentiality obligations.

---

## 6. DURATION OF CONFIDENTIALITY

Confidentiality obligations under this NDA survive for three (3) years from the date of disclosure, unless:

- The information becomes public domain (through no breach of this NDA)
- Disclosure is required by law (as provided in Section 3(e))
- Both Parties agree in writing to release the obligation

---

## 7. NO OBLIGATION TO DISCLOSE

Nothing in this NDA obligates either Party to disclose any Confidential Information. Parties are free to decline disclosure and to limit the scope of information shared.

---

## 8. NO LICENSE OR RIGHTS GRANTED

Disclosure of Confidential Information does not:
- Grant any license or rights to patents, trademarks, copyrights, or other IP
- Create any obligation to enter into a business relationship
- Establish any partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship
- Obligate either Party to proceed with any transaction

---

## 9. DISCLAIMER

**AS-IS DISCLOSURE:** All Confidential Information is disclosed "as-is" without warranty of any kind, including accuracy, completeness, or fitness for a particular purpose. The Disclosing Party makes no representations regarding the Confidential Information.

**NO LIABILITY:** Neither Party is liable for any errors, omissions, or inaccuracies in the Confidential Information disclosed.

---

## 10. REMEDIES

The Parties acknowledge that breach of this NDA may cause irreparable harm that cannot be adequately remedied by monetary damages. Accordingly, in addition to any other remedies available at law or equity, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief to prevent breach or continued breach of this NDA.

---

## 11. NO OBLIGATION TO CONTINUE DISCUSSIONS

Either Party may terminate discussions regarding the Purpose at any time without liability or further obligation to the other Party. Confidentiality obligations remain in effect even after discussions cease.

---

## 12. RESIDUAL KNOWLEDGE

However, this exception does not permit Elevra to disclose Client's specific strategic plans, financial data, customer lists, or other clearly identified Client Confidential Information.
