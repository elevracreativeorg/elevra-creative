## 4. TONE & VOICE

### Elevra's Core Voice Attributes

**All projects embody Elevra's five immutable voice attributes:**

1. **Commanding** — Authoritative, decisive, clear. We lead conversations.
2. **Elevated** — Refined, polished, intentional. Quiet sophistication.
3. **Strategic** — Purposeful, intentional. Rooted in business outcomes.
4. **Visionary** — Forward-thinking, innovative. Anticipating market direction.
5. **Results-Driven** — Outcome-focused, accountable, specific. No decoration.

---

### Project-Specific Tone Adjustments

*How does this brand's tone shift or emphasize these attributes for this specific project?*

**Commanding intensity:** [High/Medium/Low]
- For this brand, we lead with authority and strategic thinking. We don't ask questions in marketing copy; we state truths.

**Elevation level:** [Very elevated/Elevated/Accessible elevated]
- For this brand, language is sophisticated but not pretentious. We assume audience intelligence and taste.

**Strategic emphasis:** [High/Medium/Low]
- We constantly anchor messaging in business outcomes. "Why" is always clear.

**Visionary emphasis:** [High/Medium/Low]
- We position Kura as anticipating trends in luxury hospitality (personalization, sustainability, local engagement).

**Results-driven emphasis:** [High/Medium/Low]
- We speak in specifics: occupancy rates, guest satisfaction, pricing power. No vague promises.

---

### Tone Examples

*Show how this tone sounds in actual copy.*

**Headline (commanding, strategic):**
> "Luxury isn't about excess. It's about intention."

**Body copy (elevated, visionary):**
> "Every service is a strategic choice. Every space is designed with purpose. Every stay is anticipating what you'll value."

**CTA (commanding, results-driven):**
> "Reserve your curated luxury experience."
