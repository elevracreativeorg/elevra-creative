# Sales Playbook
## End-to-End Sales Process for Elevra Creative

**Elevra Creative**

---

## PURPOSE

This Sales Playbook outlines Elevra's complete sales process from lead identification through close. It ensures that every team member follows the same strategic approach, maintains Elevra's brand voice, and qualifies prospects rigorously to build a healthy pipeline of premium-tier clients.

---

## SALES PHILOSOPHY

**We don't compete on price. We compete on strategic rigor, integrated capability, and measurable results.**

Our sales approach reflects this:

1. **Lead with strategy, not services.** We talk about business outcomes first, creative execution second.
2. **Qualify rigorously.** We turn away low-fit prospects. A $50K deal with a tire-kicker is worse than a $100K deal with an ideal client.
3. **Position premium pricing as an investment, not a cost.** Our clients invest in strategic excellence. We explain why that ROI is worth it.
4. **Build relationships, not transactions.** We're not interested in one-off projects. We're interested in becoming a trusted strategic partner.
5. **Respect their time and intelligence.** No pitch decks. No marketing fluff. Direct, valuable conversation.

## STAGE 1: PROSPECT IDENTIFICATION

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Company Size:**
- Mid-market to large (10–500+ employees)
- Or high-net-worth individual founders/operators

**Industries (Primary Target):**
- Luxury hospitality
- Luxury real estate
- Oil and gas
- Pharmaceuticals
- High-end wellness
- Medical concierge
- Premium professional services

**Decision-Maker Profile:**
- C-suite (CEO, CMO, COO, Founder)
- Business-sophisticated but not necessarily marketing-fluent
- Control budget ($50K–$200K+ annual spend on creative/marketing)
- Motivated by business outcomes (ROI, competitive positioning, revenue impact)
- Respect for strategic expertise and premium quality

**Buying Signals:**
- Recent funding or capital infusion
- New market entry or expansion
- Acquisition or merger (integration needs)
- Leadership change (new CMO, CEO)
- Rebranding announcement (competitor positioning shift)
- Website redesign talk (digital transformation)
- Public statement about growth or market shift

### Where to Find Prospects

**Referral Network (Highest Priority):**
- Existing clients (ask for referrals)
- Partner agencies (complementary services)
- Professional networks (industry events, mastermind groups)
- LinkedIn connections and warm intros

**Targeted Outreach:**
- LinkedIn (research + personalized outreach to ICP profiles)
- Industry events and conferences
- Competitive intelligence (watch for competitor movements)
- Google News alerts (for announcement signals)
- Website visitor tracking (if website is live)

**Inbound (Website, Content):**
- Website inquiries and contact form submissions
- Content-driven leads (blog, case studies, LinkedIn content)
- Referral mentions and brand awareness

**NOT Recommended:**
- Cold email lists (low ICP fit, poor conversion)
- Advertising (we're not a high-volume business)
- Cheap marketplace platforms
- Price-shopping directories

### Qualification Checklist Before Outreach

Before reaching out, confirm:
- [ ] In our target industry or customer profile
- [ ] Decision-maker is reachable (direct contact or warm intro possible)
- [ ] Likely budget ($50K+) based on company size and market
- [ ] Recent activity signal (expansion, funding, leadership change)
- [ ] No red flags (distressed, reputation issues, industry headwind)

## STAGE 2: INITIAL OUTREACH

### Outreach Strategy

**Goal:** Generate a 15–30 minute discovery call.

**Format:** Personalized email or LinkedIn message (not cold call, not mass email).

**Tone:** Commanding, elevated, respectful. You're solving a problem they have, not asking for a favor.

### Outreach Email Template

Subject line should be specific, benefit-focused, not generic:

**Bad:** "Let's chat about branding"
**Good:** "Brand positioning for [Industry] market entry"

---

**Email Template:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I came across [Company]'s [specific recent news: expansion, new hire, funding, market move] and thought of you.
>
> We work with [similar companies] on positioning and brand systems that drive measurable competitive advantage in premium markets. When I saw your move into [market], I thought—"They're going to need a distinct brand position to stand out."
>
> It might not be the right time, but if you're thinking about how your brand shows up in this new market, I'd love a 20-minute conversation. No deck. Just strategic thinking.
>
> [Link to calendar or phone number for scheduling]
>
> [Your name]
> Elevra Creative

---

**Why this works:**
- Shows you did research (credibility)
- Specific to their situation (not generic)
- Leads with strategic value (not services)
- Low friction ("20 minutes")
- Confident tone (you're not desperate; you know your value)

### Follow-Up Sequence

If no response:

1. **Email 1 (Initial outreach)** — As above
2. **Email 2 (After 1 week)** — Soft follow-up: "Quick follow-up on the note I sent. Your [expansion/move] is smart—let's chat."
3. **Email 3 (After 10 days)** — Last attempt: "I'll leave this here. If the timing changes, I'd love to connect."

**Don't:** Send more than 3 emails. Move on to next prospect.

## STAGE 4: PROPOSAL DEVELOPMENT

### After Discovery: Proposal Decision

**Good fit? → Proposal**
**Uncertain fit? → Follow-up conversation**
**Poor fit? → Gracious pass**

### Proposal Approach

**Our proposals are strategic documents, not service menus.**

**Proposal structure:**
1. **Executive Summary** (1 page)
   - Situation: What we heard
   - Challenge: The strategic/business challenge
   - Our approach: How we'll solve it
   - Investment & timeline

2. **Strategic Approach** (2–3 pages)
   - Situation analysis
   - Strategic recommendations
   - Why these recommendations (business-focused reasoning)

3. **Scope of Work** (Deliverables matrix)
   - Specific deliverables
   - Timeline and milestones
   - What's included/not included

4. **Investment & Terms** (Payment schedule, timeline, approval process)

5. **About Elevra** (Who we are, why we're different)

**Tone:** Strategic, commanding, results-focused. Not glossy. Not apologetic. Confident.

**Length:** 8–12 pages maximum. Clean, professional design.

### Pricing in Proposals

**Strategy:** Always present as a range. Never apologize for premium pricing.

**Bad:** "Unfortunately, this will cost $X"
**Good:** "Strategic investment: $X–$Y"

**Bad:** "We hope you'll find this affordable"
**Good:** "This is a premium engagement. Here's why it's worth it: [ROI, competitive advantage, outcomes]"

**Positioning Premium Price:**

> "Our investment in this engagement reflects the strategic depth and integrated expertise required. Unlike vendors competing on hourly rates, we're structuring this as a strategic partnership with measurable business outcomes. Here's what justifies the investment: [specific outcomes, timeline, team, scope]."

## STAGE 5: PROPOSAL PRESENTATION

### Delivery Method

**Best:** Live call (30–45 min) where you walk through proposal together.
**Acceptable:** Email with invitation to discuss.
**Don't:** Email alone without conversation.

### Proposal Call Script

**Opening:**

> "Thanks for reviewing the proposal. Before we dive into details, I want to make sure the overall approach resonates. Did the strategic recommendations align with how you're thinking about the challenge?"

**Walk through 3 key sections:**
1. Strategic approach (make sure they buy the strategy)
2. Deliverables (what they'll get)
3. Investment (why it's worth it)

**Ask for feedback:**

> "What questions do you have? And be honest—if something doesn't feel right, I want to know."

**Handle objections:** (See section below)

**Close:**

> "Does this feel like the right approach? If yes, next step is you review internally with your team and let me know if any adjustments are needed. We aim to kick off [date]."

## STAGE 6: NEGOTIATION & CLOSE

### Common Objections & Responses

**Objection: "Your price is higher than [competitor]."**

Response:
> "I appreciate you shopping around. Most agencies are competing on hourly rates or project discounts. We're competing on strategic rigor and integrated execution. You get [specific outcomes, team depth, process]. If another firm quotes lower, they're either understaffing or underestimating scope. I'd rather be upfront about investment than underpromise and overdeliver."

**Objection: "Can you reduce the price?"**

Response:
> "I understand the impulse to negotiate, and I want to be direct: we've already quoted based on the scope and our team depth. If price is the limiting factor, we can discuss reducing scope—maybe starting with [smaller phase]. But I won't compromise the quality of team or depth of strategy to hit a lower number. What's your constraint—budget or timing?"

**Objection: "We'd like to start smaller—just logo/website/social."**

Response:
> "I hear that. Most clients want to start tactical. But here's the issue—without strategic foundation (brand architecture, positioning), a new logo or website is just decoration. It doesn't move the business forward.
>
> Here's what I'd propose: Let's start with [strategic phase: positioning + brand architecture, $X]. Once you see the strategic framework, you'll want the [visual/digital] execution, and we can discuss timing on that.
>
> Or, if budget is constrained, let's discuss what [smaller strategic scope] looks like."

**Objection: "We need this in [unrealistic timeline]."**

Response:
> "A comprehensive brand system takes 6–8 weeks minimum to do it right. If you need it in [shorter timeframe], we can accelerate, which affects cost and team capacity. What's the hard deadline, and why?"

(Then evaluate if you can actually deliver or if it's a bad fit.)

**Objection: "We need to get [committee/stakeholder] involved."**

Response:
> "Absolutely. Let's schedule a call with [stakeholder]. Here's what I'd recommend: [You/decision-maker] and I align on approach, and then we present to the broader team. Too many cooks in the approval kitchen slows everything down.
>
> Does that work?"

### Closing the Deal

**When to close:**

- They've asked specific questions about scope
- They've agreed with the strategic approach
- They've confirmed budget and timeline are realistic
- They've expressed intent ("This looks great" / "I want to move forward")

**Close script:**

> "Based on our conversation, I think this is the right approach and we're the right partner for you. Are you ready to move forward?
>
> Here's what happens next: You sign the MSA and SOW. We schedule a kickoff call. We set up access and gather initial materials. We start [kickoff date]."

**If hesitation:**

> "What's holding you back? Is it the approach, the investment, timeline, or something else?"

(Address the real objection, don't move on.)

## SALES METRICS & TRACKING

### Key Metrics to Track

- **Pipeline Value:** Total value of all open opportunities
- **Conversion Rate:** % of proposals that close (target: 60–75%)
- **Average Deal Size:** Average engagement value (target: $50K–$150K)
- **Sales Cycle:** Days from discovery call to signature (target: 15–21 days)
- **Win/Loss Rate:** % of opportunities won vs. lost

### CRM / Tracking System

Use [your CRM tool: Pipedrive, HubSpot, Notion, etc.] to track:
- Prospect name and company
- Contact info and decision-makers
- Stage (prospect, discovery call, proposal, negotiation, closed)

## VOICE & TONE IN SALES

Embody Elevra's five voice attributes in every interaction:

**Commanding:**
- Set the agenda and tone
- Push back politely if they're thinking tactically
- Be confident about your approach

**Elevated:**
- Speak to them as a peer
- Avoid jargon; explain concepts clearly
- Use sophisticated language

**Strategic:**
- Always connect to business outcomes
- Ask good questions to understand their situation
- Recommend what's right, not what's biggest

**Visionary:**
- Help them see where the market is going
- Position brand as a business asset
- Lead them toward the future state

**Results-Driven:**
- Emphasize ROI and measurable outcomes
- Specify what success looks like
- Be accountable to outcomes

## RED FLAGS: WHEN TO WALK AWAY

Don't chase prospects who are:
- ❌ Comparing purely on price (not a good fit for premium agency)
- ❌ Disrespectful of your time (late, distracted, dismissive)
- ❌ Lack clear decision authority (too many cooks, unclear budget)
- ❌ Want massive scope for tiny budget
- ❌ Don't understand or value strategic thinking
- ❌ Have unrealistic expectations (instant results, free work, etc.)

**Remember:** A bad client will cost you more than you earn in the deal.

---

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

**❌ Talking about services before understanding their challenge**
Say: "Tell me about your situation" first. Services come later.

**❌ Presenting a proposal without a pre-call**
Always present proposals in conversation, not email. Shows respect.

**❌ Apologizing for premium pricing**
Say: "This is a significant investment. Here's why it's worth it."
Don't say: "We hope this isn't too expensive."

**❌ Saying yes to everything in discovery**
Be honest about fit. A bad fit is worse than no deal.

**❌ Giving discounts to close**
If they can't afford it at list price, they'll resent you later. Hold your price.

**❌ Moving fast without alignment**
Slow down. Make sure they understand and agree with strategy before kickoff.
