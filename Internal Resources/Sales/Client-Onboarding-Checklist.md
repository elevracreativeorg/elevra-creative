# Client Onboarding Checklist
## Post-Contract to Project Kickoff

**Elevra Creative**

---

## PURPOSE

This checklist ensures every new client has a smooth, professional onboarding experience. It covers everything from contract execution through kickoff meeting, setting clear expectations and enabling seamless project delivery.

**Owner:** Enrique (Operations Director)
**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks from contract signature to kickoff
**Responsibility:** Project Manager (with Enrique oversight)

---

## PRE-KICKOFF ACTIVITIES (Days 1–5)

### Day 1: Contract Received & Signed

- [ ] **MSA & SOW received** — Confirm both documents are signed by Client and Alexandria
- [ ] **File storage created** — Set up project folder in shared drive (`Clients/[ClientName]/`)
- [ ] **Create project in CRM/PM tool** — Log engagement in Asana/ClickUp with timeline and team
- [ ] **Assign Project Manager** — Who's the primary contact for day-to-day work?
- [ ] **Brief team** — Share client overview and project scope with team

**Owner:** Project Manager
**Timeline:** Same day as signature

---

### Day 2–3: Welcome Materials & Access Provision

- [ ] **Send Welcome Email** — See template below
  - Warm greeting from Alexandria (if appropriate, personalize)
  - Introduce Project Manager and team
  - Attach Welcome Packet (see below)
  - Confirm kickoff date/time
  - Set communication expectations (response times, channels)

- [ ] **Provision platform access:**
  - [ ] Shared drive or cloud storage access (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive)
  - [ ] Project management tool (Asana, ClickUp, Notion)
  - [ ] Communication platform (Slack, email, or other)

  - [ ] Email: Send access invites with clear instructions

- [ ] **Create onboarding doc** — Share Google Doc or Notion page with:
  - Project timeline and milestones
  - Team member contact info
  - Communication protocols
  - What Client needs to provide (brand assets, stakeholder interviews, feedback process)

**Owner:** Project Manager + IT/Operations support
**Timeline:** By end of Day 3

### Day 4: Stakeholder Confirmation & Pre-Kickoff Materials

- [ ] **Confirm kickoff attendees** — Email Client listing:
  - Elevra team attending (names, titles)

  - Agenda
  - Time, date, link

- [ ] **Gather pre-kickoff materials** from Client:
  - [ ] Brand guidelines (if they exist)
  - [ ] Previous brand strategy or positioning documents
  - [ ] Website content and messaging
  - [ ] Competitor analysis or market research (if available)
  - [ ] Photography/video assets to use in project
  - [ ] Key messaging talking points
  - [ ] Organizational chart or team structure
  - [ ] Any questionnaires or briefs to complete

**Send email:** "To prepare for our kickoff, please gather the attached materials..."

**Owner:** Project Manager
**Timeline:** Day 4 (submit by 24 hours before kickoff)

## KICKOFF MEETING (Day 5–7)

### Kickoff Meeting Agenda (90 minutes)

**Facilitator:** Alexandria (if strategic project) or Project Manager

**Attendees:**
- Elevra: Project Manager, Project Lead/Designer, at least one strategic lead (Alexandria for first meetings)
- Client: Sponsor/decision-maker, other key stakeholders

---

#### **Segment 1: Welcome & Alignment (10 min)**

**Goal:** Build confidence, set tone, align on what success looks like

**Script:**

> "Thanks for being here. We're excited to get started. Before we dive into the work, I want to make sure we're aligned on what we're solving and what great looks like.
>
> This project matters because [reason we learned in discovery]. We're going to help you [strategic outcome]. Here's what we'll do..."

- Briefly restate the challenge and opportunity
- Recap our recommended approach
- Set expectation: This will require your time and feedback (but we'll make it easy)

---

#### **Segment 2: Project Overview & Timeline (15 min)**

Walk through the SOW:
- [ ] Deliverables (show examples if relevant)
- [ ] Timeline (milestone dates)
- [ ] Revision process (how many rounds, how to submit feedback)
- [ ] Approval workflow (who signs off on what)
- [ ] Success metrics (what we're measuring)

**Key message:** "This isn't a 'surprise at the end' project. You'll see work regularly. You'll provide feedback. Together, we'll refine until it's perfect."

---

#### **Segment 3: Team Introductions & Roles (10 min)**

- **Alexandria:** Founder, creative director, brand voice authority, final approval
- **[Project Manager]:** Day-to-day contact, project coordination, timeline management
- **[Project Lead]:** Leads strategy or design work, your strategic partner for decisions
- **[Other roles]:** Designers, developers, whoever will be doing the work

**Key message:** "You have direct access to the whole team. If you need something, reach out."

---

#### **Segment 4: Process & Communication (15 min)**

Clarify:
- **Communication channel:** Email, Slack, scheduled calls?
- **Meeting cadence:** Weekly check-ins? Bi-weekly? Ad-hoc?
- **Feedback submission:** How do they provide feedback? (Shared doc, email, call?)
- **Approval process:** Who approves what? How do they sign off?
- **Response times:** We'll respond within [X hours]. We expect feedback within [X days].
- **Tools:** Show them how to use Figma, project management tool, or whatever they'll be using

**Key message:** "We respect your time. We'll be efficient. But we need your engagement and feedback to make this work."

---

#### **Segment 5: Discovery Questions (30 min)**

This is the deep-dive conversation on strategy. Ask:

**Brand/Strategic (if applicable):**
- "Walk me through your ideal customer. Who are they and what do they value?"
- "What's your competitive advantage that we haven't talked about yet?"
- "In one year, what do you want to be famous for in your market?"
- "What's one thing that must be true about your brand for this to be a success?"

**Messaging (if applicable):**
- "What's the most important thing you want your brand to communicate?"
- "What do you want to be known for that you're not known for today?"
- "What's the biggest misconception about your business?"

**Design/Visual (if applicable):**
- "Show me 3 brands or designs you love and why"
- "What visual direction feels right to you?"
- "What should we absolutely not do?"

**Organization/Access:**
- "Who do we need to loop in for feedback?"
- "Who's the final decision-maker?"
- "Are there any sensitive internal dynamics we should know about?"

**Logistics:**
- "What's your preferred communication style?"
- "Are there any busy periods where you won't be available?"
- "What's your timeline for providing feedback?"

**Owner:** Project Lead or Alexandria
**Duration:** 25–30 minutes of quality Q&A

---

#### **Segment 6: Success Criteria & Expectations (10 min)**

Summarize what success looks like:

> "Here's what we're committing to: [Strategic outcome]. Here's what we need from you: [Their commitment]. If we do this right, [result]."

**Set expectations on:**
- Deliverable quality and scope
- Timeline (realistic, not aspirational)
- Your role (they provide feedback, we make decisions)
- Post-launch support (what's included, what's not)

## COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL (Ongoing)

### Weekly Status Updates

Every Friday (or per agreed cadence):
- Send status email to Client
- Include: % complete on each deliverable, any blockers, next week's plan
- Example format:

> **Project Status — Week [X]**
>
> **Completed:**
> - Competitor analysis & positioning framework
>
> **In Progress:**
> - Visual identity concepts (70% done)
>
> **Next Week:**
> - Present design concepts for feedback
>
> **Questions/Blockers:**
> - Need stakeholder feedback on messaging direction (can we schedule 15-min call?)
>
> [Link to shared project status doc]

---

### Feedback Loop

**Client provides feedback:**
- Via shared doc, email, or call (whatever was agreed)
- In writing (not scattered across conversations)
- Within agreed timeline (typically 5 business days)

**Elevra responds:**
- Acknowledge feedback
- Clarify any questions
- Update deliverables based on approved feedback
- Present revised work on schedule

**Revision process (2 rounds typically):**
- Round 1: Client provides feedback on concepts
- Elevra refines based on feedback
- Round 2: Client reviews refined work
- Final approval

## HANDOFF & LAUNCH SUPPORT

### Pre-Delivery Checklist (1 week before final delivery)

- [ ] **QA review** — Design, copy, code all reviewed for quality
- [ ] **File organization** — All deliverables organized, named, and version-controlled
- [ ] **Documentation** — Usage guides, brand guidelines, technical specs
- [ ] **Training materials** — How to use the work (if applicable)
- [ ] **Final approval call** — Walkthrough of final deliverables, get sign-off

### Delivery Day

- [ ] **Final deliverables packaged** — Organized folder with:

  - Link to all files and documentation

## VOICE & TONE IN ONBOARDING

Every Client touchpoint reflects Elevra's voice:

- **Commanding:** You lead the project. You set expectations clearly. You're confident about the process.
- **Elevated:** Professional, polished communication. No corporate jargon. Speak to them as a peer.
- **Strategic:** Always explain the "why" behind decisions and process steps.
- **Visionary:** Help them see the future state. Build excitement for the work.
- **Results-Driven:** Be specific about what success looks like and how you'll measure it.

---

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

❌ **Over-communicating:** Too many emails, calls, and updates creates noise. Stick to agreed cadence.

❌ **Under-communicating:** If something is delayed or changed, tell them immediately. No surprises.

❌ **Unclear approval process:** Make sure they know exactly who approves what and when.

❌ **Scope creep without pushback:** If they ask for extra work, document it and discuss fee impact.

❌ **Missing stakeholders in kickoff:** If key decision-makers aren't there, loop them in immediately after.

❌ **Assuming they understand the process:** Explain everything explicitly. What's obvious to you isn't to them.
