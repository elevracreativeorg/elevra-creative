# Discovery Call Script & Questionnaire
## Prospect Qualification & Strategic Intake

**Elevra Creative**

---

## PURPOSE

The Discovery Call is Elevra's first strategic conversation with a prospect. Its objective is to:

1. **Qualify the prospect** — Confirm they're in our target market (premium industries, high-net-worth decision-makers, C-suite)
2. **Understand the business challenge** — What's driving the conversation now, not just what they think they need
3. **Assess strategic fit** — Does Elevra align with their goals and budget
4. **Set expectations** — Educate them on how Elevra works (strategic, not transactional)
5. **Build trust** — Position Elevra as a strategic partner, not a vendor

---

## CALL PREPARATION (Before the Call)

### Pre-Call Research

- **Company overview:** Size, revenue (if available), key markets, publicly stated strategy
- **Leadership:** Prospect's role, background, LinkedIn profile
- **Recent news:** Funding, launches, expansions, leadership changes
- **Online presence:** Current website, branding quality, social media activity
- **Competitive context:** Who are their competitors; where do they stand in the market
- **Prospect source:** How did they find Elevra (referral, website, LinkedIn, etc.)

### Conversation Set-Up

- **Confirm attendees:** Who from Elevra (typically Alexandria or Sales Lead) and who from Client side
- **Time frame:** 45–60 minutes (respect their time; don't overrun)
- **Agenda:** Send brief agenda via email 24 hours before call
- **Materials:** Have Elevra case studies, website link, and pricing reference available
- **Environment:** Quiet location; professional background if video call

---

## DISCOVERY CALL AGENDA

### SEGMENT 1: RAPPORT & CONTEXT (5–7 minutes)

**Goal:** Build trust, get them comfortable, set the tone that Elevra is different (strategic, commanding, elevated).

**Script Opening:**

> "Thanks for taking the time today. I'm [Name], and I lead [role] at Elevra Creative. We're a brand partnership agency in Houston—we architect brand systems that drive market position and revenue for executives like you in luxury hospitality, real estate, and premium industries.
>
> Before we dive in, I want to understand your business and what's prompting the conversation. This call is about learning whether there's a fit—not about pitching services. Does that work?"

**Build Rapport:**
- Acknowledge something specific from research ("I saw your recent expansion into Dallas—impressive move")
- Ask one personal question ("How long have you been in this role?")
- Show you've done homework (but not in a stalker-y way)

---

### SEGMENT 2: BUSINESS CONTEXT (10–12 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their business situation, not just surface-level problem.

#### Question Block 1: Business Overview

**Ask:**

> "Walk me through your business briefly. What does [Company] do, who's your core customer, and what's your market position?"

**Listen for:**
- Company maturity (startup, growth, scale, mature)
- Market position (category leader, challenger, niche)
- Growth trajectory (expanding, plateauing, challenged)
- Leadership awareness of brand perception

**Follow-ups (ask 1–2 based on response):**

- "You mentioned targeting high-net-worth [audience]. How does your current brand and positioning communicate that value to them?"
- "If I asked your top 10 customers to describe your brand in one sentence, what would they say?"
- "What's changed in your market in the last 18 months?"

---

#### Question Block 2: Why Now?

**Ask:**

> "What's prompting the conversation today? Why are you exploring brand and creative work now?"

**Listen for:**
- Urgency signals (new market entry, competitive threat, launch, rebrand, acquisition)
- Pain points (brand confusion, poor conversion, team scaling, weak positioning)
- Triggering event (new hire, new funding, loss of a major customer)
- Strategic initiative (diversification, premium shift, market expansion)

**Why this matters:**
- New market entry = positioning urgency (we lead with strategy)
- Weak brand perception = architecture need
- Scaling = operational efficiency value prop
- Acquisition = integration/consolidation need

**Follow-up:**

> "On a scale of 1–10, how urgent is this? And what happens if you don't address it in the next [X months]?"

---

#### Question Block 3: Previous Work & Current State

**Ask:**

> "Have you worked with agencies or creative teams before? If so, what worked and what didn't?"

**Listen for:**
- Agency experience (previous bad experience, high expectations, or new to outsourcing)
- What they value (strategy vs. execution, speed vs. perfection, partnership vs. vendor)
- Relationship preferences (hands-on vs. hands-off, frequent check-ins vs. autonomy)

**Ask if they've done branding work:**

> "Have you done brand work before—strategy, identity, positioning? What was that experience?"

**Listen for:**
- Sophistication level (understand brand architecture or purely tactical)
- Investment level (DIY, low-cost, high-end)
- Satisfaction ("It helped" vs. "It was a waste" vs. "We never activated it")

---

### SEGMENT 3: THE ASK (8–10 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand what they think they need and what they're actually trying to solve.

#### Question Block 1: Project Vision

**Ask:**

> "Imagine we're having a conversation one year from now and the work we did together was exactly what you needed. What would be true about your brand and business?"

**Listen for:**
- Aspirational outcomes (market perception shift, pricing power, team clarity, market entry success)
- Measures of success (revenue growth, customer perception, internal alignment, competitive positioning)
- Scope hints (full rebrand, positioning only, website only, or comprehensive system)

**Why this matters:**
- This tells you if they want one-off assets or a strategic system
- This reveals what success looks like to them (should inform your proposal)

#### Question Block 2: Specific Deliverables (if relevant)

**Ask:**

> "When you think of the creative work that needs to happen, what comes to mind? A new logo? Website? Full brand system? Help me understand what you see."

**Listen for:**
- Tactical thinking ("new logo") vs. strategic ("we need to reposition to premium")
- Scope understanding
- Assumptions about what's needed

**Gently educate if needed:**

> "A lot of our clients come in thinking they need a new logo, but what they really need is a brand architecture that justifies a premium price point. Have you thought about it that way?"

---

#### Question Block 3: Team & Stakeholders

**Ask:**

> "Who else from your team needs to be part of this conversation? And who needs to sign off on decisions—is that you?"

**Listen for:**
- Decision authority (is the prospect the decider or are they influencing a committee?)
- Stakeholder alignment (sales vs. product vs. finance might have different priorities)
- Red flags (too many cooks, unclear authority, stakeholder misalignment)

**Follow-up if needed:**

> "If we move forward, would you be the primary point of contact for approvals and strategy direction?"

---

### SEGMENT 4: AUDIENCE & MARKET (5–7 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their customer and where Elevra can add strategic value.

#### Question Block 1: Customer Profile

**Ask:**

> "Describe your ideal customer. Who are you trying to reach, and what does success look like with them?"

**Listen for:**
- ICP clarity (well-defined vs. vague)
- Customer sophistication (do they understand value or are they price-shopping?)
- Customer journey (how do they find you, how do they decide, how do they perceive your brand?)

**Follow-up:**

> "How much does your brand perception influence their decision to work with you?"

---

#### Question Block 2: Competitive Context

**Ask:**

> "Who are your main competitors, and how is your brand different from theirs?"

**Listen for:**
- Competitive awareness (do they know who they're competing against?)
- Differentiation clarity (do they articulate it clearly?)
- Market positioning (are they a category leader or challenger?)

**Follow-up if weak answer:**

> "That's actually something we could help clarify—positioning against competitors is one of our core strengths. What does your positioning look like today?"

---

### SEGMENT 5: BUDGET & TIMELINE (5–7 minutes)

**Goal:** Gauge realistic budget and timeline. Don't get cheap-shopped. Qualify on investment level.

#### Question Block 1: Budget Range

**Ask:**

> "Thinking about investment, do you have a budget range in mind for this engagement?"

**Script if they hesitate:**

> "I ask because our services span a wide range—from positioning-only work ($15–25K) to comprehensive brand architecture plus website implementation ($50–150K+). Most of our clients in premium industries invest in the full system because the ROI is substantial. Do either of those ranges feel realistic?"

**Listen for:**
- Budget alignment with scope
- Willingness to invest in strategy vs. just asking for discounts
- If below typical range, they may not be qualified (competing on price, not value)

**Red flag responses:**
- "We want premium but have a $5K budget" → Usually not a good fit
- "Can you give us a discount?" (before understanding scope) → Not understanding value-based pricing

**If budget is low:**

> "I appreciate transparency. The work you're describing typically requires [X investment]. If budget is a constraint, we could start with [smaller scope], but I don't want to propose something that underdoes your business. Would it be worth exploring after you secure more budget?"

---

#### Question Block 2: Timeline

**Ask:**

> "When are you looking to have this completed by? Is there a hard deadline?"

**Listen for:**
- Urgency (month vs. quarter vs. flexible)
- Realistic understanding of work (3-week brand system is red flag for them)
- Is deadline business-driven or arbitrary?

**If timeline is unrealistic:**

> "A comprehensive brand system typically takes 6–8 weeks once we kick off. If you need it sooner, we can accelerate, but that affects cost and team capacity. When would you realistically be able to invest the time and approvals this needs?"

---

### SEGMENT 6: FIT ASSESSMENT & CLOSING (5–7 minutes)

**Goal:** Assess fit, position Elevra, and set next steps clearly.

#### Your Fit Assessment

As they talk, you're assessing:

- ✓ Are they in our target market? (Premium industry + decision-maker quality)
- ✓ Do they have real budget and decision authority?
- ✓ Are they a strategic thinker or purely tactical?
- ✓ Would Elevra's approach (strategic, results-driven, premium positioning) align with their needs?
- ✓ Do we want this business, or would it be a bad fit?

---

#### Question Block 1: What They're Looking For in a Partner

**Ask:**

> "Beyond the work itself, what are you looking for in an agency partner? What matters most to you in the relationship?"

**Listen for:**
- Strategic vs. executional (do they want thinking or just implementation?)
- Hands-on vs. autonomous (do they want to be involved or trust us?)
- Results-focused (do they care about ROI and business outcomes?)
- Culture fit (do they value excellence, accountability, strategic rigor?)

---

#### Question Block 2: Elevator Pitch & Positioning

**Now you speak.** Share why Elevra is different:

> "Here's why I think we could be a good fit: We don't design logos. We architect brand systems—positioning, strategy, visual identity, digital implementation—as an integrated platform that drives measurable business results.
>
> Most agencies treat branding as decoration. We treat it as a business asset. We lead with strategy—understanding your market, your competitive position, your ideal customer—and then everything else flows from that.
>
> What you're describing—[summarize their challenge]—is exactly where we add the most value. And our approach is operationally efficient; you're not paying for bloated overhead. You're paying for strategic rigor and premium execution.
>
> That said, we're selective about the clients we work with. It's important that we're the right partner for you."

**Why this positioning:**
- Educates them on Elevra's approach (strategic, not vendor-like)
- Emphasizes ROI and measurable outcomes
- Subtly sets expectation of premium pricing (not cheap)
- Positions Elevra as confident and selective (makes you more valuable)

---

#### Question Block 3: Next Steps

**If they seem like a good fit:**

> "I think there could be something here. Here's what I'd propose: We'd send you a proposal outlining our recommended approach, the deliverables, timeline, and investment. You'd have [X days] to review and ask questions. Then we'd schedule a call to align on strategy and answer any concerns.
>
> Does that timeline work for you?"

**If they seem like a bad fit (budget too low, not strategic thinkers, unclear authority):**

> "Thanks for the conversation. I want to be honest—I don't think we'd be the best fit for what you're describing, and I'd rather be upfront than waste your time. [Reason: 'You're looking for tactical work quickly and on a tight budget; we're built for strategic, comprehensive partnerships.']
>
> If your situation changes or you want to explore a larger engagement in the future, I'd love to reconnect. In the meantime, [suggest alternative if appropriate: 'You might have better success with a smaller design firm focused on quick turnarounds.']"

**If unclear:**

> "You've given me a lot to think about. I want to explore this a bit more before proposing anything. Can I follow up with a few questions and we can schedule a follow-up conversation in [X days]?"

## VOICE & TONE GUIDANCE

Throughout the Discovery Call, embody Elevra's voice:

- **Commanding:** You set the agenda and direction. You ask good questions and aren't afraid to push back if they're thinking tactically when they need strategy.
- **Elevated:** Use sophisticated language, avoid jargon unless explaining it. Speak to them as a peer and principal.
- **Strategic:** Always connect the dots to business outcomes. "This matters because..."
- **Visionary:** Help them see where the market is going and how brand positioning influences that.
- **Results-Driven:** Emphasize ROI, measurable outcomes, and accountability. No fluff, no decoration.

**Tone:**
- Conversational but professional
- Curious and engaged (ask follow-up questions)
- Confident (not arrogant)
- Respectful of their time
- Honest about fit (don't sell them something they don't need)

---

## RED FLAGS (When to Walk Away)

- ❌ Budget is 50%+ below your estimate for scope
- ❌ Multiple cooks in the kitchen with no clear authority
- ❌ They're primarily shopping for the cheapest option
- ❌ They don't respect your time (late to call, distracted, rude)
- ❌ They want massive scope with tight timeline and small budget
- ❌ They're comparing you purely on price to cut-rate agencies
- ❌ No clear business outcome or measurement of success
- ❌ They're seeking a vendor relationship, not a partner

**Remember:** Bad fits become bad clients. Your time is your most valuable asset. Be selective.

## FOLLOW-UP AFTER CALL

**Within 24 hours:**
1. Send thank-you email reiterating key points and next steps
2. If moving to proposal: outline timeline for when they'll receive it
3. If not moving forward: send gracious pass email or schedule follow-up

**Email template (if good fit):**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for making time today. I really appreciate your insight into [Company]'s position in the market and what you're looking to accomplish.
>
> As discussed, we'll send you a proposal outlining our approach to [strategic challenge] and the investment required. You should have that by [date]. Once you've reviewed it, we can schedule a call to align on strategy and answer any questions.
>
> In the meantime, if you have questions, don't hesitate to reach out.
>
> Talk soon,
> [Name]
