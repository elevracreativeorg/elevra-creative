# Employee Onboarding Checklist
## Day 1 through Day 30 Structured Plan

**Elevra Creative**

---

## PURPOSE

This checklist ensures every new hire (employee or contractor) has a smooth, clear onboarding experience from Day 1 through the end of their first month. It covers tools access, cultural integration, role-specific training, and expectations setting.

**Owner:** Enrique (Operations Director)
**Manager:** Direct manager/team lead
**Timeline:** 30 days
**Key Documents to Prepare:** CLAUDE.md, Department README, IP Agreement, Role Description

---

## DAY 1: WELCOME & ONBOARDING BEGINS

### Morning (First 2 hours)

- [ ] **Welcome from Alexandria** — Founder greeting (in-person if possible, or video call)
  - Warm welcome and company vision
  - Why we hired you
  - What success looks like in your role
  - What we value as a company (strategic thinking, excellence, client focus)
  - 15 minutes

- [ ] **Manager meets 1-on-1** — 30 minutes
  - Welcome to the team
  - Team overview (who you'll work with)
  - First week agenda
  - Questions about role

- [ ] **Office tour** — 30 minutes
  - Facilities overview
  - Kitchen, bathroom, parking
  - Emergency exits
  - Technology refresher

### Mid-Morning (10:30–12:00)

- [ ] **Paperwork & admin** — 30 minutes
  - [ ] Sign employment contract (if employee)
  - [ ] Sign IP & Work-for-Hire Agreement
  - [ ] Sign handbook acknowledgment
  - [ ] Complete tax forms (W-4/W-9)
  - [ ] Complete emergency contact form
  - [ ] Direct deposit setup (if employee)

- [ ] **System access & tools training** — 30 minutes
  - [ ] Password manager setup (1Password, LastPass, etc.)
  - [ ] Email access and setup
  - [ ] Slack workspace overview
  - [ ] How to access documentation and resources
  - [ ] VPN setup (if remote)

### Afternoon (1:00–5:00)

- [ ] **Team introductions** — 2 hours
  - [ ] Meet each team member (15 min 1-on-1 per person)
  - [ ] Department head introduces team structure
  - [ ] Understand who does what

- [ ] **Elevra context** — 1 hour
  - [ ] Watch or read: Company overview
  - [ ] Read: CLAUDE.md (start with Company Overview section)
  - [ ] Read: Brand Voice & Messaging
  - [ ] Review: Department README for your team
  - [ ] Review: Role description

- [ ] **Informal lunch/coffee with team** — Throughout day (informal, not forced)

### Day 3: Department & Systems Training

**Note:** Training is department-specific. Customize based on role.

**For all roles:**
- [ ] **Project management system** (1 hour)
  - [ ] How projects are tracked
  - [ ] What tools we use
  - [ ] How to log time (if applicable)
  - [ ] Status reporting expectations

- [ ] **Communication protocols** (30 min)
  - [ ] Slack channels and expectations
  - [ ] Email guidelines
  - [ ] Response time expectations
  - [ ] Meeting norms

- [ ] **File & asset management** (30 min)
  - [ ] Folder structure overview
  - [ ] File naming conventions
  - [ ] Version control (GitHub for developers)
  - [ ] Where to find resources

**For design/brand/creative roles:**
- [ ] **Design tools tour** (2 hours)
  - [ ] Figma workspace overview
  - [ ] Component library and design system
  - [ ] How projects are organized
  - [ ] Collaboration workflow

**For developer roles:**
- [ ] **Development environment setup** (2–4 hours)
  - [ ] GitHub repository access
  - [ ] Local development environment configuration
  - [ ] Code standards and conventions review
  - [ ] First simple task/PR to practice workflow

**For all roles:**
- [ ] End-of-day debrief with manager — Any blockers? Questions?

---

### Day 4: Client Delivery & QA Standards

- [ ] **Deliverable standards** (2 hours)
  - [ ] Review Service Specs (what we deliver and quality bar)
  - [ ] Walk through a completed project (design/code/content)
  - [ ] Understand revision process and approval workflow
  - [ ] Quality expectations

- [ ] **Client communication** (1 hour)
  - [ ] How we talk to clients (review CLAUDE.md messaging)
  - [ ] Client communication protocols
  - [ ] What can you approve, what needs escalation

- [ ] **Role-specific deep dive** (2–3 hours)
  - Manager walks through typical project workflow for this role
  - Shows examples of what good looks like
  - Walks through a project from start to finish

---

### Day 5: First Task Assignment

- [ ] **Small, doable first task** (4–6 hours, supervised)
  - [ ] Manager assigns a small, low-risk project or task
  - [ ] Manager available for questions
  - [ ] Not expected to be perfect, but to understand the process
  - [ ] Goal: Build confidence, not assess performance

- [ ] **End-of-week debrief** (1 hour, Friday afternoon)
  - [ ] How's your first week going?
  - [ ] What's clear? What's confusing?
  - [ ] Any concerns or questions?
  - [ ] Next week preview

## WEEK 2–3: DEEPER LEARNING & INDEPENDENT WORK

### Week 2 Goals

- [ ] **Complete foundational training:**
  - [ ] All department-specific training completed
  - [ ] All tools and systems understood
  - [ ] Team relationships established
  - [ ] CLAUDE.md fully read and understood

- [ ] **Start real work:**
  - [ ] Assigned to first real project (still supervised/reviewed)
  - [ ] Understand project workflow from intake to delivery
  - [ ] Begin producing work (design, code, strategy, content)

### Training by Department

**Brand/Strategy:**
- [ ] Positioning framework workshop (2 hours with Strategist)
- [ ] Competitive analysis process (1 hour)
- [ ] Messaging development walkthrough (1 hour)
- [ ] Review past brand projects (2 hours)

**Design:**
- [ ] Design system deep dive (2 hours)
- [ ] Component creation workshop (2 hours)
- [ ] Design critique norms (1 hour)
- [ ] Design review process (30 min)

**Development:**
- [ ] Codebase tour and architecture (3 hours)
- [ ] Testing & QA process (1 hour)
- [ ] Deployment process (1 hour)
- [ ] First code review (supervised PR)

**Content/Production:**
- [ ] Content calendar review (1 hour)
- [ ] Photography/video direction process (1 hour)
- [ ] Content approval workflow (30 min)
- [ ] First content assignment (supervised)

### Ongoing

- [ ] **Weekly 1-on-1 with manager** (30 min every week)
  - How's it going?
  - What's working? What's hard?
  - Feedback on early work
  - Adjust training based on needs

- [ ] **Friday team meetings** (if applicable)
  - Attend team standup or weekly sync
  - Start to participate (even if mostly listening)

- [ ] **Peer mentorship** (ongoing)
  - Pair with peer on real projects
  - Learn by doing alongside experienced team member

## WEEK 4: INTEGRATION & EXPECTATIONS

### Final Training Elements

- [ ] **Company policies & benefits** (1 hour)
  - [ ] Time off and PTO policy
  - [ ] Remote work expectations
  - [ ] Expense and reimbursement policy
  - [ ] Professional development budget
  - [ ] Benefits (health insurance, 401k, etc., if applicable)

- [ ] **Performance expectations** (1 hour with manager)
  - [ ] What success looks like in first 90 days
  - [ ] Feedback process and timing
  - [ ] How often you'll check in
  - [ ] When formal feedback comes

- [ ] **Longer-term learning plan** (30 min with manager)
  - [ ] Skills you'll develop in first 6 months
  - [ ] Courses, certifications, or training opportunities
  - [ ] Mentorship relationships
  - [ ] Growth trajectory

### End-of-Month Check-In (Day 30)

**30-minute meeting with Manager:**

- [ ] **Feedback from Manager:**
  - What's going well?
  - What needs improvement?
  - Specific feedback on work quality and collaboration

- [ ] **Questions from New Hire:**
  - Any lingering questions about role or company?
  - Anything that would help you be more successful?
  - Feedback on onboarding experience (what helped, what didn't)?

- [ ] **Next 60 Days:**
  - What projects will you own?
  - What skills are you developing?
  - When's the next formal feedback?

## VOICE & CULTURE IN ONBOARDING

Every interaction reinforces Elevra's culture:

- **Commanding:** We're confident about who we are and what we do. New hires should feel the strategic clarity and decision-making authority.
- **Elevated:** Onboarding is professional and polished. No messy process.
- **Strategic:** Everything ties to "why" — why we do things this way, why culture matters, why brand voice is important.
- **Visionary:** Help new hires see where we're heading, not just where we are.
- **Results-Driven:** Set clear expectations. Measure. Give feedback.

---

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ **Overwhelming on Day 1:** Too many people, meetings, and information at once. Spread it out.

❌ **No clear first task:** New hires need something concrete to do, not just training. Give them a real task by Day 5.

❌ **Inconsistent messaging:** If onboarding talks about brand voice but projects don't reflect it, culture feels fake.

❌ **No feedback:** Don't wait 90 days to tell them how they're doing. Check in after first week.

❌ **Ignoring questions:** Every "dumb question" is a chance to reinforce culture and expectations.

❌ **Making them figuring it out:** Guidance is clear, not cryptic. "Go figure it out" doesn't work for onboarding.
